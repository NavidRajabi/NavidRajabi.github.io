My name is Navid Rajabi. I am a second year PhD student in Computer Science department (with the focus on AI) at George Mason University. My research interest lies at the intersection of Computer Vision (CV) and Natural Language Processing (NLP), using Deep Learning techniques with the aim of bridging the gap between CV and NLP. This research area can be considered as a foundation for a wide spectrum of use cases, ranging from image captioning to video understanding, summarization and captioning, and Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN).

 

Before joining George Mason University, I was a graduate research assistant at University of San Francisco and Illinois State University, where I received my master of science degree. Also, I was appointed as the Vice President of the Honor Society chapter at Illinois State University in 2017. During my master's degree, I was mainly working on the applications of Machine Learning and Deep Learning in Internet of Things (IoT) to enhance the performance and robustness of the Smart Buildings/Cities architectures. Prior to that, I received my bachelor of science degree in Computer Engineering (Software Engineering branch) from KNTU.
